Small business broadband grants worth £3000

According to the Telegraph the UK Government is bringing back the broadband voucher scheme! This means small UK business and large can invest again in the hard to reach areas to finally get connected. It’s hard to believe the UK was already planning on a fibre roll-out in the late 80s…

The 2017 broadband voucher scheme initiative is to increase speeds and get business online with better or any connection at all. It’s a shame to see the UK fall behind other countries when it comes to broadband converge- for a long time we were the pioneers in telephony, it was the UK that was on the cusp of providing super fast fibre broadband rolled out in 1986 as Dr Cochrane (who used to work as BT’s Chief Technology Officer explains in his interview with techradar.

In 1986, I managed to get fibre to the home cheaper than copper and we started a programme where we built factories for manufacturing the system. By 1990, we had two factories, one in Ipswich and one in Birmingham, where were manufacturing components for systems to roll out to the local loop.

Unlike the UK who held back our plans to invest in broadband in the 80, Countries like Korea and Japan chose to invest, and the stats show that the investment paid off. According to the OECD, the average download speeds for the UK in 2014 were 9.9Mbit/s compared to Japan which enjoys an average download speed of 14.6Mbit/s and Korea at 23.6Mbit/s. It’s high time we pulled out socks up and allow our country to flourish on a level footing. Faster broadband makes doing businesses easier, faster and opens new markets. It’s about time we forget about the race to the bottom on broadband prices, invest in our infrastructure and reap the benefits of faster broadband for our small business economy.
